Early Career

* Worked as a dishwasher and street musician to buy his first Gibson guitar.

* 1966: Formed Santana Blues Band with David Brown, Marcus Malone, and Gregg Rolie.

* Caught the attention of Bill Graham, promoter of the Fillmore Auditorium.

* Influenced by 1960s cultural icons and the hippie movement in San Francisco.

Breakthrough and Success (1969–1972)

* Signed by Columbia Records; band name shortened to Santana.

* Performed at Woodstock (1969) — standout performance of “Soul Sacrifice” launched their fame.

* Debut album Santana (1969) reached #4 on the Billboard 200.

* Hit single: “Evil Ways” (suggested by Bill Graham).

* Internal creative tensions emerged between rock-oriented and fusion-oriented members.
